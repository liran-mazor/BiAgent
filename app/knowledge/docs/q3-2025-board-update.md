## Executive Summary

H1 2025 closed at **$1.954M** — 1.6% above the $1.924M H1 target ($918K Q1 + $1.05M Q2 → wait, actual Q1 came in at $884K and Q2 at $1.07M = $1.954M total). The site-wide discount ban held — this is the first H1 in company history without a promotional event, and Q2 showed that customers will pay full price in season.

H2 forecast: $2.70M. Full-year forecast revised up to **$4.65M** (vs $4.60M board commitment). The board discussed three items: the Books B2B milestone, the EMEA feasibility question, and the Sports buying strategy for H2.

### Books B2B milestone
Target was 6 new B2B corporate clients by June 30. **Actual: 9 new clients signed**, bringing total to 23. The March reading list campaigns generated inbound from 14 prospects; 9 converted. This is the most significant commercial development in the first half. Combined B2B revenue in H1: $156K — run rate implies ~$310K+ for the full year, which would be a new record.

CFO note: B2B clients have a 0.4% return rate and pay net-30 with no promotions. The contribution margin on B2B Books is approximately 68% vs 58% for retail Books. Every incremental B2B client is worth roughly 3x an equivalent retail customer on a margin basis.

### Sports Q1 miss — timing, not demand
The Q1 Sports miss ($226K vs $240K target in H1; mostly Q1-driven) was a timing issue. New Year fitness demand peaked around January 12 — two weeks earlier than our Q1 budget assumed. By the time our January email campaign went out (January 15), the peak had passed. The team caught this in February and front-loaded the March outdoor prep campaign. March Sports was $52K vs $48K target — recovered.

Action: 2026 Sports New Year campaign to launch January 8, not January 15.

### Sports H2 buying risk
This is the board-level concern. The team bought $150K of H2 Sports inventory in June (top of the approved $150K H2 budget). Q3 back-to-school youth sports is expected to be the key demand window (August–September). If this sub-category underperforms, we will have excess summer inventory entering Q4 with no natural sell-through window.

**CFO recommendation:** Hold $40K of the H2 buying budget until August 15. Only release if July Sports revenue hits $68K target. Board approved this approach.

**If inventory backs up:** authorized up to 22% markdown on outdoor equipment after October 1 to clear before year-end. Margin impact would be approximately $18K.

## Key Decisions

### Decision 1 — Books Subscription Box Pricing
The subscription box is at 920 members — 77% of the 1,200 year-end target. Growth has slowed (added 340 members in H1, pace needed for target is 280 in H2). Two options presented:

**Option A:** Hold price at $32/month and invest $30K in digital marketing.
**Option B:** Price reduction to $28/month to accelerate growth, then raise back to $34/month in Q1 2026.

Board voted 4–1 for **Option A**. Rationale: price reductions are hard to undo. The $32/month price anchors the perceived value of the box. If we miss the 1,200 target slightly, that is acceptable — hitting 1,050–1,100 is still a successful launch year.

### Decision 2 — EMEA Feasibility Study
The CEO raised the EMEA opportunity for the first time formally. The rationale: Books and Electronics have strong demand signals in UK e-commerce. Our existing supplier relationships have UK distribution capability. A limited pilot (UK only, Books + Electronics, no Sports or Home) could launch in H2 2026 with low capital requirements.

Board discussion: Three board members expressed support for a structured feasibility study. Two wanted to wait until after FY 2025 closes and 2026 guidance is set.

**Decision:** Approved a feasibility study to be completed by November 2025. Study to cover: UK addressable market, fulfillment costs, VAT/customs complexity, competitor landscape, and a capital requirement estimate for a 12-month pilot.

CEO to present study findings at the December 2025 board meeting. No capital commitment made at this meeting.

### Decision 3 — No Q3 or Q4 Site-Wide Promotional Events
CFO proposed testing one promotional event in Q3 (September) to assess the demand sensitivity of the discount ban. Board voted 3–2 to **decline** — the signal from H1 is that customers are adapting to full-price, and testing a discount now would contaminate the data. The Q4 Black Friday pages (which are product-specific, not site-wide) remain approved.
