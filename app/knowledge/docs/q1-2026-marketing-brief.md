## Overview

Q1 2026 marketing is a low-spend quarter by design. No January-wide promotion — the 2026 Annual Plan explicitly removed the January sale that historically trained customers to wait. March is the primary activation window: one category-specific push in Sports (New Year fitness tail-off) and one in Books (reading season).

Total Q1 marketing budget: **$48,000** — down from $62,000 in Q1 2025. The reduction reflects the shift away from broad discount-led campaigns toward retention and subscription growth.

---

## Channel Breakdown

### Email & CRM
- Budget: $8,000
- Focus: reactivation of lapsed customers (last purchase > 120 days). Target 3,200 reactivations at an assumed $28 AOV.
- Books subscription renewal sequence: three-email cadence starting week 4 of February. Goal: reduce subscription churn from 6.2% to 4.5% in Q1.

### Paid Search
- Budget: $22,000
- Sports: capture New Year fitness intent through January. Wind down by February 10. No bidding on generic "gym equipment" — margin is too thin. Focus: branded + specific product terms.
- Electronics: minimal spend. No category promotion planned. Maintain baseline brand search coverage only.

### Social & Influencer
- Budget: $11,000
- Books: partner with 4 mid-tier reading influencers (50K–200K followers) for February "reading season" push. Fixed-fee, no performance component.
- Sports: one influencer activation in January tied to New Year resolution content. Budget cap: $3,500.

### Affiliate
- Budget: $7,000
- Commission structure unchanged from 2025: 6% on Electronics, 8% on Books and Sports, 4% on Clothing.
- No new affiliate recruitment in Q1 — focus on activation of existing partners.

## Dependencies and Risks

**Dependency:** Sports inventory position must be confirmed by January 8. If the end-of-season markdown from H2 2025 left significant overstock, the New Year fitness campaign budget may be reallocated to Clothing to avoid promoting low-margin units.

**Risk:** The decision to remove the January sale creates downside risk in January revenue. The 2026 Annual Plan accounts for this — January is projected at the lowest monthly revenue of the year ($290K). Marketing should not be pressured to fill this gap with unplanned spend.

**Risk:** Books influencer partners contracted for February are fixed-fee. If the reading season campaign underperforms, there is no performance-based adjustment available. This is a deliberate choice to avoid performance-pressure dynamics with editorial influencers.
