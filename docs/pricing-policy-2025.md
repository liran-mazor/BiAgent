## Overview

This document defines pricing rules, gross margin floors, and discount authority for all product categories. It supersedes the 2024 Pricing Policy (Version 2.0) introduced in January 2024.

All pricing decisions must comply with these rules. Exceptions require CFO approval for items below gross margin floors and CEO + CFO approval for any discount exceeding the limits defined in this policy.

This policy does **not** cover B2B corporate account pricing (governed by the B2B Playbook) or EMEA pricing (to be defined upon pilot launch).

---

## Gross Margin Floors by Category

These are minimum acceptable gross margin percentages for any product listed on the site. Products falling below these floors must be repriced, delisted, or approved as a named exception.

| Category | Gross Margin Floor | Notes |
|----------|-------------------|-------|
| Electronics | 22% | Cost-plus minimum. No exceptions without CFO sign-off. Price floor: no Electronics SKU listed below $49. |
| Clothing | 45% | Markdown policy (see below) applies after 90 days. Floor applies to original list price. |
| Home | 40% | Bundle discount may bring effective margin below 40% — acceptable at bundle level if individual SKUs are above 40%. |
| Books (retail) | 60% | B2B bulk orders are excluded — governed by B2B Playbook. |
| Sports | 38% | Seasonal markdown allowed at end-of-season (see below). |

### Margin Floor Exceptions
Any SKU proposed at below the floor must be submitted to the CFO with a written justification. Acceptable reasons:
- Supplier cost increase requiring temporary below-floor listing while repricing is finalized (maximum 30 days)
- End-of-season clearance (must also satisfy markdown policy)
- New category entry pricing (maximum 6 months, with board awareness)

## Discount Policy

### Site-Wide Promotional Events
The board explicitly banned site-wide promotions effective January 2025. No discount that applies automatically to all products is permitted without CEO and board approval.

- **Permitted:** Product-specific or category-specific promotions
- **Permitted:** Loyalty-priced reorder emails (Clothing and Books only)
- **Permitted:** Bundle discounts within defined limits
- **Not permitted:** Site-wide percentage-off codes
- **Not permitted:** Homepage banner discounts applying to the full catalog
- **Not permitted:** "Flash sale" events covering more than 20 SKUs unless pre-approved

The Black Friday event is the single exception — product-specific Electronics and Books deals are approved annually by the CFO. The Black Friday plan must be submitted to the CFO by October 15.

### Category-Specific Markdown Policy

**Clothing:**
- Up to 20% discount after 90 days in inventory
- Up to 40% discount after 180 days in inventory
- No discount on new arrivals (first 30 days)
- Size-run closeouts (less than 3 units remaining in a size) may be discounted up to 50% without approval

**Home:**
- Bundle discount capped at 8% off the sum of individual prices
- No clearance discounting on items below 4 units — delist instead
- Furniture clearance requires CFO approval if discount exceeds 25%

**Electronics:**
- No discounts on items listed for less than 14 days
- Maximum 15% promotional discount without CFO approval
- Commodity devices (phones, tablets) may only be discounted if manufacturer wholesale price has decreased

**Books:**
- No discounts on individual retail titles
- B2B bulk orders: 5–15% discount on orders of 25+ copies; 15–25% on 100+ copies (B2B Playbook)
- Subscription box price changes require CFO approval

**Sports:**
- In-season: no discounts unless defective or significant inventory overhang (>60 units)
- End-of-season clearance: up to 30% after the season ends (outdoor/summer: September 30; winter sports: March 31)
- If end-of-season clearance does not clear inventory, a second markdown of up to 45% is authorized after 30 additional days

### Discount Authority Matrix

| Discount Level | Authority |
|---------------|-----------|
| Up to 15% (category-specific) | VP Sales |
| 15–25% | CFO |
| 25–40% | CEO + CFO |
| Above 40% (clearance only) | CEO + CFO + board notification |
| Any site-wide discount | CEO + CFO + board approval |

## Bundle Pricing

Bundles are two or more products sold together at a combined price below the sum of individual prices.

- Maximum bundle discount: **8%** for Home and Clothing; **5%** for Electronics; **10%** for seasonal Sports bundles; **12%** for Books curated sets (approved Q1 2025 for reading-list bundles only).
- Bundle price must still respect the gross margin floor for each included product.
- "Free shipping" on bundles is a logistics incentive, not a price discount, and does not count toward the discount percentages above.
- No bundles that include items from more than two categories (cross-category bundling requires VP Sales approval).

---

## Dynamic Pricing

Dynamic pricing was piloted on 30 Electronics SKUs in Q3 2024 and expanded to the full Electronics catalog in Q3 2025.

Rules:
- Maximum intraday price increase: 12% above list price
- Maximum intraday price decrease: 18% below list price (subject to margin floor)
- Price changes must not result in the same customer seeing two different prices within a 24-hour window
- Dynamic pricing does not apply to Clothing, Home, or Sports (under evaluation for Clothing accessories in 2026)

---

## Price Increases

Planned price increases must be:
1. Communicated to affected customers with 30 days notice if they have items in their wishlist or cart
2. Applied on a Tuesday or Wednesday (never Friday–Sunday when support is reduced)
3. Logged in the pricing system with effective date and reason

2025 approved increases:
- Books: 4% average catalog increase (implemented February 1, 2025)
- Electronics proprietary brand: 6% increase (implemented Q2 2025)

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can we run a "clearance" page with 25% off everything?**
A: No. A clearance page covering all products is a de facto site-wide promotion and requires CEO + board approval. A clearance page showing only items that individually qualify for their category markdown policy is permitted.

**Q: A VIP customer asked for a custom discount. Is that allowed?**
A: For retail customers, individual discounts up to 10% may be offered by Customer Success for service recovery purposes only (delayed shipments, damaged items). Not for relationship pricing. For B2B clients, refer to the B2B Playbook.

**Q: Can we match a competitor's price?**
A: Price matching is not a standing policy. Requests must be escalated to VP Sales. Price matching below the gross margin floor is not permitted under any circumstances.

**Q: Sports is 20% above the season-end date. Can we start markdown?**
A: No. The season-end date is the trigger, not inventory levels. Starting early reduces full-price sell-through and sets a customer expectation of early discount. Hold to the policy dates.
