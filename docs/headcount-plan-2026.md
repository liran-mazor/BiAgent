## Overview

2026 headcount plan: **net +4 FTEs** over the year. Current total: 23 FTEs. Target end-of-year: 27 FTEs. Hiring is back-half weighted — Q1 and Q2 are focused on retaining and optimizing the current team. Q3 hiring is tied to the EMEA pilot launch.

## 2026 Hiring Plan

### Q2 2026 — 2 Hires
**1. Senior Software Engineer (Backend)**
- Role focus: platform reliability and order management system
- Compensation range: $130K–$145K base
- Start date target: April 1
- Rationale: Engineering is at capacity. The EMEA pilot requires localization of the order pipeline (VAT handling, UK address format, GBP currency) which is estimated at 6 weeks of focused engineering effort. Current team cannot absorb this without impact to core roadmap.

**2. Customer Support Lead**
- Role focus: team lead for the 3-person support team, owns tooling and process
- Compensation range: $72K–$82K base
- Start date target: May 1
- Rationale: Support volume grew 28% in 2025 driven by Books subscription inquiries and Sports returns. The current team is reactive. A lead role establishes process, owns the Zendesk configuration, and manages the contractor pool used during peak periods.

### Q3 2026 — 2 Hires (EMEA-Linked)
**3. UK Country Manager (Part-time, Contractor)**
- Role focus: UK market entry — supplier relationships, local compliance, initial customer acquisition
- Compensation: £4,500/month contractor rate (GBP)
- Start date target: July 1 (aligned with EMEA pilot launch)
- Rationale: Not a permanent hire. The December 2025 board decision was to pilot EMEA before committing to a permanent UK headcount. This contractor covers the pilot period (Q3–Q4 2026). Decision to convert to FTE made at the December 2026 board review based on UK pilot results.

**4. Data Analyst**
- Role focus: revenue analytics, cohort analysis, pricing optimization support
- Compensation range: $88K–$98K base
- Start date target: August 1
- Rationale: Finance is currently producing all revenue reporting manually. As 2026 revenue targets increase to $5.5M across 5 categories and a new market, manual reporting becomes a risk. This role owns the analytics stack, produces weekly revenue dashboards, and supports the pricing policy review cycle.

## Compensation Philosophy

### Merit Increases (2026)
- Merit pool: 4% of base salaries, distributed in April performance reviews
- Allocation: 0%–6% range. Below-target performers: 0–2%. On-target: 3–4%. Above-target: 5–6%.
- No automatic COLA adjustment in 2026. Merit pool is the only salary movement mechanism.

### Benefits
- Health: company covers 80% of individual premium, 60% of family premium (unchanged from 2025)
- Retirement: 3% employer match on 401(k), vesting over 3 years (unchanged)
- Equity: not applicable — privately held, no equity program

### Contractor Policy
- Contractors engaged for work exceeding 6 months require CFO approval
- No contractor may be engaged at a rate exceeding 1.4x the equivalent FTE fully-loaded cost without CEO approval
- Contractors do not participate in merit reviews or benefits

## Key Risks

**Hiring timeline risk:** The Senior Backend Engineer role requires specific experience (distributed systems, UK localization) that is scarce. If we miss the April 1 start date, the EMEA engineering work will be delayed by one full quarter, pushing the pilot from Q3 to Q4. The Board should be informed if hiring for this role is not confirmed by March 15.

**Retention risk:** Two senior engineers are approaching the 3-year mark in Q2 2026. Vesting cliff creates retention risk. Engineering lead to conduct stay interviews in February. If either role is at risk, budget authority to offer off-cycle merit increases of up to $10K is pre-approved by the CEO and does not require Finance sign-off.

**Scope creep on EMEA contractor:** The UK Country Manager role is scoped as 6 months, pilot-only. There is historical precedent in this company for contractor-to-FTE conversions happening earlier than planned. Finance must be included in any scope extension conversation before a commitment is made to the contractor.
